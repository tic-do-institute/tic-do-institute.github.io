About the Institute

TIC-DO Institute is an independent research organization dedicated to the computational modeling of biological systems. We investigate the mechanisms of maladaptive stability and phase transitions in the human nervous system through the lens of the Free Energy Principle and Active Inference.

Our primary focus is to elucidate how informational dynamics influence somatic regulation, specifically identifying the computational failures that lead to "frozen" metastable attractors in physiological states.

Current Research

Informational Perturbation Reverses Precision Collapse in a Metastable Proprioceptive Inference Regime Takafumi Shiga
Under Review

This study characterizes a mechanistically specified failure mode of active inference, termed Proprioceptive Prediction Error Neglect (PPEN). Using a multi-study framework (RCT, crossover, and ecological validation, N=106), we demonstrate that these states behave as metastable attractors driven by precision collapse and can be modulated via discrete informational phase transitions.
